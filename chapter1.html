༺ The First Step into Murim ༻

For factories workers at small and medium companies, playing games was a basic skill.

Of course, depending on the person, there were some that they didn’t acknowledge what he played as games. By that, they meant mobile phone applications that only involved automatic hunting, in-app purchases, and gacha could not be recognized as an actual game.

However, for factory workers, these games held a value beyond just being games.

Weren’t they the reliable genre that allowed one to level up solidly even while the phone was running inside a cabinet?
