༺ The First Step into Murim ༻

For factories workers at small and medium companies, playing games was a basic skill.

Of course, depending on the person, there were some that they didn’t acknowledge what he played as games. By that, they meant mobile phone applications that only involved automatic hunting, in-app purchases, and gacha could not be recognized as an actual game.

However, for factory workers, these games held a value beyond just being games.

Weren’t they the reliable genre that allowed one to level up solidly even while the phone was running inside a cabinet?

While one's salary might not increase, the joy of leveling up was still by their side.

Moreover, these games accounted for over 70% of the conversation among team members, so it could be said that it was more akin to their modern social life than mere gaming.

However, when limited to PC or console games, gaming was a somewhat distant hobby for factory workers. Their hobbies were usually drinking alcohol (casually, with a meal), drinking alcohol (company dinners), drinking alcohol (with women), and if it wasn’t any of that, then at most, fishing. That was mainstream.

In that sense, his hobby of gaming was quite uncommon.

He was a diligent gamer.
